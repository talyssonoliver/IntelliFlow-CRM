## What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that a website stores in your browser. They allow
the site to remember information about your visit, such as your sign-in state,
preferred theme, or the cookie categories you have agreed to. IntelliFlow CRM
uses cookies, along with similar technologies such as local storage, to operate
the public site and the authenticated product.

Some cookies are set by IntelliFlow directly, while others are set by trusted
third-party providers that help us measure site performance or deliver the
service. All cookies used on IntelliFlow CRM are listed in the categories
section below.

## Categories We Use

IntelliFlow CRM groups cookies into four categories that mirror the options
available in the consent banner: necessary, analytics, marketing, and
preferences.

Necessary cookies support authentication, cross-site request forgery protection,
and the storage of your consent choice itself. They are always on because the
service cannot function without them. Analytics cookies help us understand how
visitors reach and move through the site so we can improve it. Marketing cookies
measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns. Preference cookies remember
choices such as language or theme so the site feels familiar on your next visit.

## How We Obtain Consent

When you visit the site for the first time, the cookie banner shown at the
bottom of the page explains that necessary cookies are already in use and asks
you to decide about analytics, marketing, and preference cookies. Nothing in
those optional categories runs until you accept. We record the version of this
policy alongside your choice so we know exactly what you agreed to.

If we materially change the categories or providers listed below, we update the
policy version and ask for a fresh choice.

## Managing Your Preferences

You can change your preferences at any time. Open the cookie banner again from
the site footer, update your category choices, and save. We will immediately
stop any categories you turn off and clear the corresponding cookies from your
browser where we can.

Most browsers also let you block or delete cookies directly. Note that blocking
necessary cookies will sign you out and prevent core site features from working.

## Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by providers we rely on. Google Analytics and Mixpanel help
us measure product usage. Meta and Google Ads help us understand the reach of
marketing campaigns. These providers process only the data described in their
own privacy notices, and their cookies only load when the matching category is
enabled.

We do not sell personal data, and we do not share cookie data with providers
other than those disclosed here.

## Retention

Cookies expire on different schedules depending on their purpose. Session
cookies disappear when you close the browser. Persistent cookies stay for the
duration listed in the cookie inventory, typically ranging from 24 hours for
short-term analytics identifiers to one year for long-term preference cookies.
Your consent record is kept for up to one year, after which we ask again.

## Your Rights

You can always withdraw consent, ask for details of how your cookie data is
processed, or request deletion by contacting our privacy team. Withdrawing
consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out beforehand. See
the Privacy Policy for broader rights under UK GDPR and other applicable
regimes.

## Contact

If you have a question about cookies, the consent banner, or a specific provider
in the inventory, email our privacy team. We will respond within the timeframe
required by the applicable law.
