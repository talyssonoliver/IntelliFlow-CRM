# Discovery Call Script

## Overview

This script provides a structured framework for conducting discovery calls with prospects. The goal is to uncover pain points, understand the business context, and qualify the opportunity using the MEDDIC framework.

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Attendees**: Prospect stakeholders, Account Executive, optionally Sales Engineer
**Outcome**: Qualified opportunity with clear next steps OR disqualification with reason

---

## Pre-Call Preparation (15 minutes)

**Research Checklist**:
- [ ] Review company website and recent news
- [ ] Check LinkedIn profiles of attendees
- [ ] Review company size, industry, tech stack (ZoomInfo, LinkedIn)
- [ ] Identify potential pain points based on industry/size
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 hypothesis-driven questions
- [ ] Review any prior interactions (emails, form submissions, content downloads)

**Set Your Objective**:
- What do you need to learn to qualify this opportunity?
- What MEDDIC elements can you uncover in this call?
- What would make this a great fit vs. not a fit?

---

## Call Structure

### 1. Opening (5 minutes)

**Build Rapport**:
```
"Hi [Name], great to meet you! Before we dive in, how's your day going?"
[Brief small talk - weather, location, shared connection, recent news about their company]

"I see you're based in [city]. Have you been with [Company] long?"
```

**Set Agenda**:
```
"Here's what I'd like to cover in our time together:
- First, I'd love to learn about you and your role at [Company]
- Then, understand what prompted you to reach out / what you're hoping to solve
- Share a bit about IntelliFlow and see if there's a fit
- If it makes sense, discuss next steps

Does that sound good? Anything you'd like to make sure we cover?"
```

**Confirm Time**:
```
"I have us down for [45] minutes - does that still work on your end?"
```

---

### 2. Understand Their Role (5 minutes)

**Questions**:
```
"Tell me about your role at [Company]. What does your day-to-day look like?"

"How long have you been in this position?"

"What are your main priorities or objectives this quarter/year?"

"Who else on your team is involved in [sales/CRM/operations]?"
```

**What You're Listening For**:
- Their level of seniority and decision-making authority
- Pain points in their daily workflow
- Strategic vs. tactical role
- Team size and structure
- Whether they're the Economic Buyer or a stakeholder

**MEDDIC Element**: Economic Buyer (identify if they control budget)

---

### 3. Understand the Business (10 minutes)

**Current State Questions**:
```
"Walk me through your current sales process. What does it look like from lead capture to closed deal?"

"What tools or systems are you using today for CRM, sales engagement, and automation?"

"How many leads do you generate per month? What's your conversion rate?"

"How big is your sales team? Any plans to scale up or down?"

"What does your tech stack look like? What systems do we need to integrate with?"
```

**Context Questions**:
```
"What's been your biggest win as a sales team in the past year?"

"If I talked to your sales reps, what would they say is their biggest frustration?"

"How much time are your reps spending selling vs. admin work?"
```

**What You're Listening For**:
- Current baseline metrics (leads, conversion, response time, etc.)
- Technology landscape and integration requirements
- Team maturity and sales sophistication
- Growth trajectory or scaling challenges

**MEDDIC Elements**: Metrics (current state), Decision Criteria (current tools)

---

### 4. Identify Pain (15 minutes)

This is the most critical part of the call. Use the SPIN framework (Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff).

**Situation Questions** (establish context):
```
"How do you currently manage incoming leads?"

"What's your process for lead assignment and follow-up?"

"How do you track pipeline and forecast deals?"
```

**Problem Questions** (uncover challenges):
```
"What's not working well with your current setup?"

"What are the biggest gaps or limitations you're facing?"

"Where are you losing leads or revenue in the current process?"

"What prompted you to start looking for a new solution now?"
```

**Implication Questions** (amplify the pain):
```
"What's the impact of [problem] on your revenue/team/growth?"

"How much time/money is [problem] costing you per month?"

"What happens if you don't solve this in the next 6-12 months?"

"How does this affect your ability to hit your sales targets?"
```

**Need-Payoff Questions** (envision the solution):
```
"If you could wave a magic wand and fix [problem], what would that look like?"

"What would be different if you solved this? How would your day-to-day change?"

"What would it mean for your business if you could [improve metric by X%]?"
```

**Example Pain Discovery Flow**:
```
Rep: "How do you currently handle inbound leads from your website?"
Prospect: "They go into a spreadsheet, and then we manually assign them to reps."

Rep: "Got it. What challenges are you running into with that process?"
Prospect: "It's slow. Sometimes leads sit there for hours before anyone sees them."

Rep: "How often does that happen?"
Prospect: "Probably 30-40% of our leads don't get touched same-day."

Rep: "What's the impact of that delay on your conversion rates?"
Prospect: "Hard to say exactly, but we know speed matters. We're probably losing deals."

Rep: "If I do the math - you mentioned 500 leads/month, 40% delayed, 15% conversion rate normally.
Even if half of those delayed leads are lost opportunities, that's potentially 30 deals per month.
At $10k average deal size, that's $300k in missed revenue. Does that sound about right?"
Prospect: "Yeah, that's a real problem."

Rep: "What would it be worth to your business to cut that response time from hours to minutes?"
```

**What You're Listening For**:
- Specific, quantifiable pain points
- Urgency (why now?)
- Impact of inaction (cost of doing nothing)
- Emotional engagement (frustrated, excited, desperate?)

**MEDDIC Element**: Identify Pain (core business problem)

---

### 5. Understand the Buying Process (10 minutes)

**Decision-Making Questions**:
```
"Have you purchased a CRM or similar software before? Walk me through how that process went."

"Who else needs to be involved in evaluating and deciding on a solution like this?"

"What's the typical approval process for a purchase of this size at [Company]?"

"Who ultimately signs off on a purchase like this? When can we get them involved?"
```

**Timeline Questions**:
```
"What's your timeline for making a decision?"

"What's driving that timeline? Is there a specific event or deadline?"

"What could slow down or speed up your decision process?"
```

**Budget Questions**:
```
"Have you allocated budget for this project?"

"What's your budget range for solving this problem?"

"If we can demonstrate clear ROI, is there flexibility to invest more to get the right solution?"
```

**Evaluation Criteria Questions**:
```
"What are the must-haves vs. nice-to-haves in a solution?"

"What would make you say 'yes, this is the one' vs. 'no, not the right fit'?"

"Are you evaluating other vendors? Who else are you looking at?"

"What concerns or risks do you have about changing CRM systems?"
```

**What You're Listening For**:
- Decision timeline (realistic or aspirational?)
- Stakeholders and decision-making roles
- Budget availability and flexibility
- Evaluation criteria and competitors
- Potential deal-killers or roadblocks

**MEDDIC Elements**:
- Economic Buyer (who signs?)
- Decision Criteria (what matters most?)
- Decision Process (steps and timeline)

---

### 6. Identify Champion (5 minutes)

**Champion Questions**:
```
"Who internally is most excited about solving this problem?"

"If we move forward, who would be the internal advocate to help drive this?"

"What's in it for you personally if this project is successful?"

"How do you typically build consensus for new tools or initiatives?"
```

**What You're Listening For**:
- Is this person willing to champion internally?
- Do they have influence with the Economic Buyer?
- What's their personal motivation?
- Are they willing to introduce you to stakeholders?

**MEDDIC Element**: Champion (internal advocate)

---

### 7. Share Your Solution (5 minutes)

**Only after you've uncovered pain should you pitch.**

**Tailored Value Proposition**:
```
"Based on what you've shared, it sounds like your biggest challenges are:
1. [Pain #1 - slow lead response]
2. [Pain #2 - manual processes eating rep time]
3. [Pain #3 - lack of pipeline visibility]

The good news is, this is exactly what IntelliFlow is built for.

[Share 2-3 minute overview tailored to their pains]

- AI-powered lead routing gets leads to the right rep in under 60 seconds
- Automation handles 80% of data entry and follow-up tasks
- Real-time pipeline dashboards give you full forecast visibility

Companies like [similar customer] have seen [specific results - 40% faster response, 25% higher conversion].

Does that resonate with what you're trying to achieve?"
```

**Keep It Brief**:
- Focus on their pains, not all your features
- Use metrics that matter to them
- Social proof from similar companies
- Save deep dive for demo

---

### 8. Next Steps (5 minutes)

**If Qualified** (all MEDDIC elements present or discoverable):
```
"This sounds like a great fit. Here's what I'd suggest for next steps:

1. I'll send you a quick recap email with what we discussed
2. Let's schedule a 60-minute demo where I can show you IntelliFlow in action,
   tailored to your [specific use cases]
3. Ideally, we'd want [stakeholders: sales manager, operations, IT] to join so we can
   address everyone's questions at once

Does that work? What's your calendar look like next week?"
```

**If More Information Needed**:
```
"I want to make sure I bring you the right solution. Before we move to a demo,
I'd love to connect with [Economic Buyer / IT / other stakeholder] to understand
[budget / technical requirements / approval process].

Can you make an introduction?"
```

**If Not Qualified**:
```
"Based on our conversation, it sounds like [reason - timing isn't right / you need X feature we don't have / budget constraints].

I don't want to waste your time. Would it make sense to reconnect in [3-6 months]
when [condition changes]? In the meantime, I'll send over [resource] that might be helpful."
```

**Schedule Next Meeting Before Hanging Up**:
- Never end with "I'll send you some times"
- Book the meeting while you have them
- Send calendar invite immediately after call

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Don't**:
- Pitch before understanding their pain
- Talk more than the prospect talks (aim for 70/30 them/you)
- Skip budget/timeline questions (feels awkward but essential)
- Accept vague answers ("we're looking to improve things")
- End without clear next steps
- Forget to identify Economic Buyer early

**Do**:
- Ask follow-up questions to get specifics
- Quantify pain with metrics whenever possible
- Listen for emotional cues (frustration, excitement, urgency)
- Take detailed notes in real-time
- Confirm next meeting before ending call
- Disqualify quickly if not a fit

## Discovery Call Scorecard

Rate yourself after each call (1-5 scale):

- [ ] Did I uncover specific, quantifiable pain? (1-5)
- [ ] Did I identify the Economic Buyer? (1-5)
- [ ] Did I understand their decision process and timeline? (1-5)
- [ ] Did I identify a potential Champion? (1-5)
- [ ] Did I learn their decision criteria? (1-5)
- [ ] Did I establish clear next steps? (1-5)
- [ ] Did the prospect talk more than I did? (1-5)
- [ ] Did I book the next meeting before hanging up? (1-5)

**Total Score**: ____ / 40

- 35-40: Excellent discovery
- 28-34: Good, some gaps to fill
- 20-27: Needs improvement
- <20: Re-qualify or disqualify
